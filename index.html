About

Bodoni Moda, designed by Owen Earl, is a recreation of the timeless Bodoni font family for the digital age. Bodoni Moda exists in Earl's digital type foundry, Indestructible Type, with other typefaces such as Marauder and Besley. Created in 2019, this typeface seeks to aid its predecessors' high-contrast model in a flexible online setting. Thick and razor-thin lines paired together looked exceptional in the eighteenth-century printing press and can now present just as much elegance today on screen.

Created in 1798 by one of the greatest typographers in the world, Giambattista Bodoni, the original typeface was born. Born into an Italian family of typographers, Giambattista trained and became the director of the Royal Typographic Office. He was a highly skilled bookmaking artist who also created other typefaces that helped shape Bodoni, such as Bodoniroman and Papale. Inspired by French typography, he spent his life creating this typeface. Crafted as a result of the industrialization of printing techniques of the time, this typeface symbolizes progress.

Bodoni was constructed with a common foundation of regularity, clarity, elegance, and beauty. The extreme contrast between thick and thin lines, hairline serifs, and methodical perpendicularity are all aspects anyone can expect to enjoy in the Bodoni typeface. This modern serif typeface transcends time and can be seen in many designer brands, album covers, and magazine covers today.
